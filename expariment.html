My Stress Management Journey

Stress acts as an accelerator: it will push you either forward or backward, but you choose which direction.

Stress, to me, is like carrying an invisible backpack that gets heavier throughout the day. It's my body and mind's response to pressure, demands, and 
challenges that feel overwhelming. Sometimes it manifests as racing thoughts at night, tension in my shoulders, or that sinking feeling when I look
 at my to-do list. In small doses, it can motivate me, sharpen my focus, and push me to accomplish things I didn't think possible. The  problem arises
 when stress becomes chronic—when that backpack never comes off.

"Stress is not what happens to us. It's our response to what happens. And response is something we can choose."

So , let's begin it a journey towards my stress life .

Stress Triggers 
* Exams and assignments: Academic pressure is a significant stressor, especially with deadlines clustering together.
* Timelines and deadlines: The pressure to complete tasks within specific timeframes.
* Family situations: Personal issues at home that can affect your mental well-being.
* Peer pressure: Social expectations that may push you in uncomfortable directions.
* Uncertainty or lack of planning: Not knowing what's coming or feeling unprepared.
* Digital distractions: How phones and social media can steal focus and create more work later.
* Time management challenges: Difficulties balancing various responsibilities.
